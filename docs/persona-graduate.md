# Lightweight Persona: Graduate Student Perspective

## 1. Persona Overview

### Name

Minghao Li

### Age

24

### Role

International graduate of JCU Singapore

### Current Situation

Minghao Li recently graduated from JCU Singapore after completing his degree as an international student.

When he first arrived in Singapore, he needed to manage many tasks within a short period of time, including preparing documents, arranging accommodation, understanding Student's Pass requirements, attending orientation, accessing university systems, and adapting to daily life.

Looking back, he believes that the main problem was not the lack of information. The information existed, but it was spread across different websites, emails, and messages. New students often found it difficult to understand which tasks were urgent and which tasks could be completed later.

---

## 2. Background

Minghao has completed the full international student journey at JCU Singapore.

During his studies, he observed that new students often asked similar questions about:

* arrival preparation;
* accommodation;
* Student's Pass formalities;
* transport options;
* orientation activities;
* JCU student systems;
* attendance requirements;
* university support services; and
* daily life in Singapore.

He believes that a simple checklist platform could reduce confusion and help new students settle in more quickly.

---

## 3. Goals

Minghao wants new international students to:

* prepare important documents before travelling;
* understand which tasks are urgent;
* avoid missing important deadlines;
* access reliable official information;
* complete tasks step by step;
* track their progress clearly;
* feel more confident during their first month; and
* avoid relying only on informal advice from friends or group chats.

---

## 4. Pain Points

Based on his past experience, Minghao identifies several common problems:

* Important information is scattered across multiple websites and emails.
* New students may receive too much information at the same time.
* Some students do not know which tasks should be completed first.
* Informal advice from classmates may be outdated or incomplete.
* Official pages may contain long or complicated explanations.
* Students may forget whether they have completed a task.
* Students often search for information on their phones, so complex pages are inconvenient.
* Students may feel stressed when they are unfamiliar with Singapore systems.

---

## 5. Needs

Minghao believes that new students need:

* a clear four-stage checklist;
* short explanations for each task;
* reliable official source links;
* a visible progress tracker;
* a mobile-friendly interface;
* an FAQ page for common questions;
* clear reminders to check official websites for updated information; and
* a platform that does not collect sensitive personal information.

---

## 6. Behaviour

Minghao usually:

* recommends checking official sources for important matters;
* shares useful links with new students;
* explains urgent tasks in a simple order;
* advises students not to depend only on group chat messages;
* uses his phone to search for practical information; and
* prefers clear checklists instead of long instructions.

---

## 7. Quote

> “When I first arrived, I had many questions and too many sources to check. A simple checklist with official links would have saved a lot of time.”

---

## 8. User Scenario

A new international student asks Minghao what should be completed during the first month in Singapore.

Minghao recommends using the Landed website.

The student opens the website and sees four stages:

1. Before Arrival
2. Arrival Day
3. First Week
4. First Month

The student selects **First Month** and views the remaining tasks.

The student marks completed items, checks official source links, and reviews the FAQ page when instructions are unclear.

Because the progress is saved in the browser, the student can return later and continue from the unfinished tasks.

---

## 9. Design Implications

| Persona Need                                       | Design Response                                     |
| -------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------- |
| New students receive too much information at once. | Divide tasks into four stages.                      |
| Students may not know which tasks are urgent.      | Show tasks in a clear priority order.               |
| Advice from classmates may be incomplete.          | Provide verified official source links.             |
| Official pages may be difficult to understand.     | Use short summaries before external links.          |
| Students may forget completed tasks.               | Save progress in the browser.                       |
| Students often use mobile phones.                  | Use a mobile-first layout with simple navigation.   |
| Students may worry about privacy.                  | Avoid collecting personal or sensitive information. |
