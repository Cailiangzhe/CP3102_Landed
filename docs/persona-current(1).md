# Lightweight Persona

## 1. Persona Overview

### Name

Buqing Wu

### Age

21

### Role

Current international student at JCU Singapore

### Current Situation

Buqing Wu is an international student currently studying at JCU Singapore. When he first arrived in Singapore, he needed to manage several tasks within a short period of time, such as preparing documents, understanding Student's Pass requirements, attending orientation, learning how to access university systems, and adjusting to daily life.

Although useful information was available, it was spread across university emails, official websites, group chats, and advice from classmates. This made it difficult to understand which tasks were urgent and which tasks could be completed later.

Buqing mainly uses his mobile phone to search for information and check important updates.

---

## 2. Background

As a current student, Buqing has already experienced the challenges of settling into Singapore.

During his first month, he needed to:

* prepare important documents;
* understand arrival procedures;
* complete Student's Pass formalities;
* attend orientation activities;
* access JCU student systems;
* understand transport options;
* find reliable official information; and
* adjust to daily life in Singapore.

He believes that new students would benefit from a simple platform that organises important tasks clearly.

---

## 3. Goals

Buqing wants new international students to:

* understand which tasks should be completed first;
* avoid missing important deadlines;
* track completed tasks;
* find clear explanations in simple language;
* access reliable official sources;
* use the platform easily on a mobile phone; and
* feel less confused during their first month in Singapore.

---

## 4. Pain Points

Based on his experience, Buqing identifies several common problems:

* Important information is spread across different websites and emails.
* Some official pages contain too much text.
* New students may not know which tasks are urgent.
* Students may forget whether they have already completed a task.
* Advice from classmates may not always be accurate or up to date.
* New students may not know which official source they should trust.
* Complicated layouts are inconvenient when using a mobile phone.

---

## 5. Needs

Buqing believes that new students need:

* a four-stage checklist;
* clear progress tracking;
* short and simple explanations;
* direct links to official sources;
* a mobile-friendly interface;
* an FAQ page for common questions; and
* a platform that does not collect sensitive personal information.

---

## 6. Behaviour

Buqing usually:

* searches for information using his mobile phone;
* checks official websites when information is important;
* asks classmates for help when instructions are unclear;
* saves useful links in browser tabs or chat messages;
* prefers short explanations before reading detailed official pages; and
* prioritises urgent tasks first.

---

## 7. Quote

> “When I first arrived, the information was available, but it was difficult to know what I should do first. A clear checklist would make the process much easier.”

---

## 8. User Scenario

A new international student asks Buqing which tasks should be completed during the first week in Singapore.

Buqing recommends using the Landed website.

The student opens the website and sees four stages:

1. Before Arrival
2. Arrival Day
3. First Week
4. First Month

The student selects **First Week**, opens the checklist, and views the urgent tasks.

After completing orientation, the student marks the task as finished. The website saves the progress in the browser.

When the student needs more information about the Student's Pass process, the student opens the relevant checklist item and follows the official source link.

---

## 9. Design Implications

| Persona Need                                     | Design Response                                                         |
| ------------------------------------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| New students do not know which tasks are urgent. | Organise tasks into four clear stages.                                  |
| Students may forget completed tasks.             | Save checklist progress in the browser.                                 |
| Official information can be difficult to read.   | Provide short explanations and official links.                          |
| Students mainly use mobile phones.               | Use a mobile-first layout with clear buttons and cards.                 |
| Advice from classmates may be outdated.          | Provide verified official source links.                                 |
| Students are concerned about privacy.            | Avoid collecting names, IDs, contact details, or sensitive information. |
